Senvix operates a four-stage predatory funnel designed to lock victim funds permanently.

(1) CELEBRITY IMPERSONATION — The scam deploys 1000 fraudulent advertisements impersonating 116 public figures. Senvix uses deepfake videos and fake testimonials featuring Cristiano Ronaldo, Jeff Bezos, Elon Musk, Ana Botín (Spanish banking executive), and Felipe VI (Spanish King). Video ads appear on Facebook, Instagram, and Google, claiming these celebrities endorse Senvix as a "guaranteed profit" trading bot. The ads are geo-targeted to specific regions, with victims in Spain, Portugal, Greece, and Poland seeing localized celebrity spokespeople like Manuel Luís Goucha, Karol Nawrocki, and Giorgia Meloni.

(2) GEOGRAPHIC TARGETING — Senvix concentrates its attack on 18 countries: Denmark, Portugal, Spain, Italy, Norway, Greece, Poland, France, Qatar, Croatia, Ireland, Czech Republic, Belgium, Japan, Germany, Finland, Australia, and Slovakia. The platform refreshes 23 new ad creatives every 7 days, maintaining ad campaign velocity for 216 consecutive days (first detected August 21, 2025; last active March 25, 2026). This geographic spread indicates professional scam infrastructure serving multiple regional markets simultaneously.

(3) THE DEPOSIT FUNNEL — Victims click the ad and arrive at a fake trading interface. They deposit funds via cryptocurrency or wire transfer. The platform instantly confirms the deposit and displays rising fake profit numbers. Victims see their account balance growing by 5-15% daily — entirely fabricated. Scammers send multiple follow-up emails and phone calls from rotating number sequences, creating urgency: "Your account is scheduled for liquidation", "Your bonus expires in 24 hours", "A live trading opportunity closes tonight." These messages exploit the psychological anchoring effect — victims convince themselves the "profits" are real and must act quickly to access them.

(4) THE WITHDRAWAL TRAP — When victims request their first withdrawal, Senvix deploys the actual trap. The platform responds with blocking messages: "Account not verified", "KYC requirements pending", "Compliance hold active". Victims are then told they must pay additional fees to "unlock" the account — ranging from EUR 500 to EUR 5,000. Alternatively, they're pressured to deposit more funds to "increase trading volume" before withdrawals are permitted. No withdrawal ever completes. Phone support becomes unresponsive. The account is permanently locked. Victims have effectively transferred their money to a criminal network with zero recovery path.
