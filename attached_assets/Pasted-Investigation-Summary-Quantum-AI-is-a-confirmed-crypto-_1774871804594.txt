How This Scam Works
Quantum AI deploys a four-stage confidence scheme targeting retail investors searching for cryptocurrency trading automation.

Stage 1: Celebrity Impersonation and Geo-Targeted Advertising. The scam uses 3076 paid advertisements impersonating 28 celebrities, deployed across 45 countries including the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, India, Brazil, Mexico, Germany, Spain, France, and Italy. Ads feature real political and business figures without consent — Elon Musk, Jeff Bezos, Bill Gates in English-speaking markets; Narayana Murthy and Mukesh Ambani in India; Keir Starmer and Cyril Ramaphosa in their respective countries. These creatives are refreshed at a velocity of 42 new advertisements every 7 days, allowing the scam to evade ad platform detection and maintain continuous reach. Each ad promises automated trading returns of 10-50% monthly with zero experience required.

Stage 2: The Funnel and Deposit Success. Victims click the advertisement and land on a fake trading dashboard. Account creation is frictionless — email, password, phone number. The platform immediately displays fake testimonials from supposed traders earning thousands daily. To activate trading, victims must deposit capital. Crucially, this initial deposit succeeds. Bank transfers, credit cards, and cryptocurrency deposits clear without issue. The victim sees the money reflected in their Quantum AI account dashboard. This instant deposit confirmation is the psychological trap — it creates false legitimacy. Victims believe the platform is real because the money is there.

Stage 3: Fake Profits and Psychological Manipulation. Over days or weeks, the victim's account dashboard displays rising balances — automated trades supposedly generating 5-15% returns daily. These are entirely fabricated. The victim never traded anything. The numbers exist only on the dashboard. Concurrently, the victim receives relentless phone calls and WhatsApp messages from changing numbers claiming to be account managers. These contacts use high-pressure tactics: limited-time bonuses, urgent account verification requirements, claims that the victim has qualified for exclusive trading algorithms. The messages instill urgency and excitement.

Stage 4: The Trap — Withdrawal Lockout and Fee Extraction. When the victim attempts to withdraw funds — whether the fake profits or their original deposit — the platform blocks the transaction. An error message appears: account verification incomplete, compliance deposit required, or trading volume threshold not met. The victim is told they must pay a unlock fee ranging from 500 to 5000 USD in cryptocurrency or wire transfer to access their money. Some victims pay once; others are told a second fee is required, and a third. None of these payments result in withdrawals. At this point, the victim has lost both their original deposit and the unlock fees. Support contact becomes impossible — email addresses stop responding, phone numbers disconnect, and the account dashboard no longer loads.

Our Investigation Methodology
This review was conducted using SpyOwl ad surveillance technology, which monitored cryptocurrency-related advertising campaigns across 50+ ad networks and social platforms between 2025-02-01 and 2026-03-26. SpyOwl detected 3076 distinct ad creatives using the brand name Quantum AI or variations thereof, deployed across 45 target countries with geo-specific celebrity impersonations. Zero results were found for any Quantum AI entity with legitimate financial services licensing. Domain registration records and SSL certificate data were inspected to identify operational infrastructure patterns and registration velocity. Payment processor integrations were identified where possible to understand capital flow mechanisms. Pattern matching and threat scoring: The collected ad data was compared against a database of 500+ known crypto investment scams to identify operational and tactical overlaps. The 95/100 threat score reflects five factors: (1) ad volume and velocity (3076 creatives, 42 per week), (2) celebrity impersonation abuse (28 without-consent endorsements), (3) geographic dispersion (45 countries targeted), (4) campaign persistence (419 days active), and (5) confirmed withdrawal blockade reports from victims across multiple countries. Key Investigation Findings
During our analysis of Quantum AI, we observed that 14 of the 3076 ad creatives (0.46%) were duplicates created within 48 hours of each other, indicating rapid creative cycling to evade ad platform deduplication systems — this velocity pattern is consistent with sophisticated scam operations, not standard marketing campaigns.
We traced Quantum AI ad creatives to 8 separate Facebook ad accounts created between January 2025 and March 2026, with 6 of those accounts created within 30 days of each other, suggesting deliberate account farming to distribute ad volume and prevent single-account shutdown.
The celebrity impersonation pattern in Quantum AI creatives shows clear geo-localization strategy: UK political figures (Keir Starmer, Sophy Ridge) appear only in GB-targeted ads, Indian business leaders (Narayana Murthy, Mukesh Ambani) appear exclusively in IN-targeted ads, and South African politicians (Cyril Ramaphosa) appear only in ZA-targeted ads. This localization indicates knowledge of regional influencers and suggests operational sophistication beyond single-market scams.
We identified that Quantum AI landing pages use SSL certificates issued within 72 hours of ad deployment in multiple instances (6 of 45 countries analyzed), indicating just-in-time infrastructure provisioning — a known evasion tactic where domains are registered, certificated, and deployed simultaneously to minimize exposure window for abuse reporting.
The withdrawal blockade pattern reported by victims across India, Brazil, Germany, and the United States uses identical excuse messaging (compliance deposit, trading volume threshold, account verification) despite being deployed through separate ad accounts and landing pages, indicating centralized fraud script management across the entire operation.
